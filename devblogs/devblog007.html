Masks and Creatures


Kevin


Late last month Kevin has made various masks based on the skill sets and play styles.

The Karasu-Tengu is an old Japanese myth. This kind of Tengu has been thought to have feathers, wings and a beak. In some paintings they look like monsters with sharp teeth and claws, like died out predatory dinosaur and in other paintings it looks like they are out of a comic. It is said that this monster has magical skills.

The Hannya mask is said to display the complexity of human emotions. If an actor wearing the mask happened to look straight ahead, the mask appears frightening and angry; but when tilted slightly down, the face of the demon appears to be sorrowful. The oldest hannya mask is dated 1558.

Kitsune mask is a mask of a fox. It has meaning in religion and folk tradition. In Japanese culture, Fox has contradictory behavior. It can be benevolent (good) or malevolent (evil) depending on the situation.

Tengu (天狗, “heavenly dog”) is a type of legendary creature from Japanese folk religion and are also considered a type of Shinto god (kami) or yōkai (supernatural beings). … Buddhism long held that the tengu were disruptive demons and harbingers of war.
